observations → Dandelions

We were out for a walk. He had been bored and overtired but I couldn’t coax him to sleep, so I thought « on va changer des idées ». It was a beautiful sunny afternoon with a strong and surprisingly cool breeze blowing from the south-east.

We circled the upper section of the fortress listening together to the birds and the breeze, all the while bathed in the late spring sunlight. He fell into a threshold state between sleep and wakefulness — his eyes fixed on the horizon, perhaps the jagged peaks of Montserrat visible in the distance. Aside from the birds and the wind, quiet.

I was in a sort of reverie myself, basking in the sensuality of being alive with a little one in peace together, when my eye happened upon some dandelions — many of them already bearing their seed-heads, lacy-white and trembling in the breeze. I plucked one from the ground and proffered it to him. His eyes glanced upon it and then up to meet my gaze with a questioning look. I brought it close to his face and blew gently, dispersing the seeds into the breeze.

His astonishment was so beautiful and pure: eyes wide, body tense and trembling with surprise. Each time more so. After a dozen or so the wonder waned slightly and we resumed the walk. He drifted back into reverie.

Life always has a gift to give if one is receptive.

Cardona, Spain, 6 May 2026
