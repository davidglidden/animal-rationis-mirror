Colophon

Authorship

The site is mine; I am answerable for it (see About for the threshold form of that claim). The work is made in the pilgrim posture (per AldineXXI Prologue): the road is the work, the writing accumulates without performance, the attentive reader recognizes the character without it being announced.

“Animal rationis capax.”
The animal capable of rationality.
— Jonathan Swift, amending Aristotle

This phrase named the difference I needed: not rational animals — the burden of perpetual reason — but creatures capable of rationality. The site explores what that capability means — how presence emerges, how patterns connect, how attention shapes both seer and seen.

Lineage

The work draws on formative voices, living and dead.

The four guides shape the daily practice:

  • Christopher Alexander: living structure emerges from patient observation; the pattern language; seek what wants to grow, not what one wants to impose.
  • Gaston Bachelard: the poetics of space; rehearsal room as shell of resonance, bow stroke as breath, silence as texture.
  • John Berger: seeing is never neutral; observation carries ethics, politics, love; to look is to be implicated.
  • Richard Sennett: craft as the dialogue between hand and material; the workshop as site of resistance and revelation.

The deeper philosophical formation:

  • Giambattista Vico: verum factum, we know what we make; civilizations think in images before concepts; corsi e ricorsi as humility before pattern.
  • Giacomo Leopardi: the Zibaldone as a mind moving through time without arriving; melancholy and tenderness as epistemological stances.
  • Robert Pogue Harrison: the human animal as caretaker of the dead; the obligation that grounds responsibility to living and unborn; the ligature (Prologue) is his.
  • Martin Heidegger: thrownness, dwelling, situatedness; held under critical tension. Harrison’s correction is part of the inheritance.

For the formative dwelling-space behind the writing, see Hearth.
