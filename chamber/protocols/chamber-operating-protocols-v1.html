Overview

The Chamber Operating Protocols define a structured framework for multi-agent philosophical deliberation, synthesizing insights from multi-agent debate research with our existing Chamber methodology. Unlike factuality-focused debate systems, the Chamber orchestrates epistemically individuated voices for symbolic, ontological, and poetic critique.

Key Distinctions from Standard Multi-Agent Debate

Aspect Standard MAD Chamber System
Goal Factual consensus Divergence mapping
Agents Undifferentiated LLMs Voice-encoded personas
Output Single answer Annotated deliberation
Critique Logical validity Epistemic multiplicity
Resolution Majority vote Editorial synthesis

Protocol Flow

Phase 1: Invocation (Round 0)

invocation:
  prompt_type: "opening"
  content: |
    [Original text or question presented]
  
  instructions: |
    Each voice approaches this text from their unique epistemic stance.
    Respond authentically to what calls to you.
    Mark any fictional works with appropriate notation (°, ~, †, §, ∞, ※).

Phase 2: Initial Response (Round 1)

Each voice provides their primary reading/response: - No awareness of other voices yet - Pure epistemic perspective - Fictional work generation encouraged - 500-1000 tokens typical

Phase 3: Cross-Voice Engagement (Round 2)

cross_voice_prompt:
  awareness: "full"  # Voices see all Round 1 responses
  
  instructions: |
    Having witnessed the other voices' responses:
    - What resonates or creates productive tension?
    - Where do you see incomplete patterns?
    - What questions emerge between perspectives?
    - How might fictional works from other voices be extended?

Phase 4: Synthetic Critique (Round 3)

synthesis_prompt:
  mode: "collective"
  
  instructions: |
    As a final round, each voice offers:
    - Essential insight that emerged from dialogue
    - Unresolved tensions worth preserving
    - Recommendations for canon development
    - Meta-observations about the deliberation itself

Phase 5: Editorial Synthesis

Human curator (David) creates: - Unified deliberation narrative - Canon entry selection - Meta-commentary (private) - Cross-reference mapping

Epistemic Safeguards

Divergence Preservation

Unlike consensus-seeking debate: - Tensions are features, not bugs - Contradictions reveal deeper patterns - No voting or averaging of positions - Editorial synthesis honors all voices

Shadow Protocol Integration

When activated, shadow voices: - Challenge aesthetic completeness - Name who/what is excluded - Refuse participation if needed - Force ethical reckonings
